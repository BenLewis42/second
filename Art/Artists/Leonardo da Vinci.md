# Leonardo da Vinci

## Overview
Renaissance polymath and artist (1452-1519). Master of observation and interdisciplinary thinking.

## Key Works
- Mona Lisa
- The Last Supper
- Vitruvian Man
- Virgin of the Rocks

## Major Insights
- **Curiosity as a Method**: His notebooks reveal obsessive observation across disciplines - anatomy, engineering, botany, hydraulics. Art and science were inseparable.
- **Drawing as Thinking**: He treated drawing not as decoration but as a research tool. Sketches were investigations into form, movement, and structure.
- **Perfection Through Study**: He believed complete understanding of a subject (including its underlying mechanics and proportions) was necessary for authentic representation.

## Philosophical Approach
- Rejected pure theory - valued empirical observation
- Believed in the unity of knowledge
- Saw nature as the ultimate teacher

## Related Concepts
- Renaissance humanism
- Sfumato technique
- Observational drawing
- Scientific illustration

## Quotes
> "Study the science of art and the art of science"
