## The University of Wisconsin's Role

- The University of Wisconsin has had a significant historical connection to the development of Social Security in the United States.
- Professor Edwin Witte, who taught at the University of Wisconsin, was the Executive Director of the committee that designed the original Social Security program in 1934.
- Another Wisconsin alumnus, Arthur Altmeyer, served as the first Commissioner of the Social Security Administration, leading the program for 16 years from 1937 to 1953.
- The State Historical Society of Wisconsin is a leading repository for records related to the history of Social Security due to the state's close ties to the program's origins.
