# Postmodernism

**Postmodernism** names a loose family of late-twentieth-century developments in **philosophy**, **literature and art criticism**, **architecture**, and **cultural theory**. It is not one doctrine; writers disagree sharply about what, if anything, the label should cover. Still, several recurring themes help orient reading.

## Themes (useful handles)

- **Suspicion of “grand narratives.”** Jean-François Lyotard famously characterized the postmodern as “incredulity toward metanarratives”—single overarching stories (progress, science-as-salvation, history-as-destiny) that claim to explain everything.
- **Language and practice.** Much post-structuralist thought treats meaning as **relational** and **historically situated** rather than anchored in fixed essences. Debate, institutions, and power shape what can be said and who is heard.
- **Power / knowledge.** [[Michel Foucault]] analyzed how disciplines, norms, and institutions produce categories of personhood and truth; that line of thought influenced how “postmodern” social critique reads modern institutions.
- **Simulation and hyperreality.** [[Jean Baudrillard]] described media and signs as sometimes displacing an ostensible “real” referent—a provocative lens for consumer culture and mass media, not a neutral sociology of communication.
- **Difference and multiplicity.** [[Gilles Deleuze]] (with Félix Guattari) offered concepts of **difference**, **rhizomes**, and **desiring-machines** that reject simple tree-like hierarchies of meaning; whether all of this should be filed under “postmodernism” is disputed, but the conversations overlap.

## Philosophy vs the arts

In **architecture and visual art**, “postmodern” often names a stylistic reaction to high modernism (historical quotation, irony, eclecticism). In **philosophy**, the term more often tracks debates about **truth**, **interpretation**, and **critique** after structuralism. The vocabularies cross-pollinate, but **art-historical postmodernism** and **philosophical post-structuralism** are not identical projects.

## Reading stance

Treat “postmodernism” as a **historical label** and a **cluster of arguments**, not a personality type or a synonym for “nonsense.” The serious work is always in specific texts and their objections—fair or unfair—to modern ideals of reason, history, and autonomy.
