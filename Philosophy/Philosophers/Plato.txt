PLATO (428-348 BCE)

Key Ideas:
- Theory of Forms: Reality exists in two realms - the material world (imperfect, changing) and the realm of Forms/Ideas (perfect, eternal)
- The Cave Allegory: Most people mistake shadows on a wall for reality; philosophy is the journey toward enlightenment
- The Good: The highest Form, source of truth and knowledge
- Justice in the Soul: Virtue comes from proper harmony of the soul's three parts (reason, spirit, appetite)

Major Works:
- Republic
- Phaedo
- Symposium
- Timaeus

Influence on Art:
- Plato was skeptical of art - viewed it as thrice removed from truth
- Artists create imitations of physical objects, which are themselves imitations of perfect Forms
- Yet his ideas about perfection, proportion, and ideal beauty have deeply influenced art theory

Connection to Art:
- Artists and architects have long sought to represent the "ideal" or "Form" behind appearances
- Renaissance artists studied Platonic ideas of beauty and proportion
